About

Hi there! I’m Rob. I also go by Robin or sometimes Robinson.

What do I do ?

I’m a Software Progressional working in the San Francisco Bay Area, California, United States. As of writing this, I’m in the 19th year of working in the Software Industry and it feels like I just got started. I worked as a Software Developer and Tech Lead for around 9 years and as an Engineering Manager for around 9 years. I love to learn new things and it has been a grand learning experience and it continues to be so every day. For work and education related details, please checkout my LinkedIn profile.

Who am I ?

I was born and brought up in Bangalore. My parents are Orthodox Christians from Kerala. I view life with colored glasses of idealism and optimism for technology that most middle-class Bangaloreans of my generation share. I love reading books and getting lost in thought while reading one. I’ve liked different genres at different times mostly based on the people that I interact with.

I also love traveling - mainly to historic landmarks, love train journeys. I also love walking long distances and hiking. I ran the SF half-marathon a couple of times and have done many local 10K runs.

I’m grateful for having been surrounded by people and places that brought out the best in me. If you knew me many years back and find me changed with better abilities and more knowledge than before, thank the people I’ve had the chance to be with. I also feel very lucky for being alive at this point in time in the history of mankind. It is arguably the best era so far.

Website

This website is a collection of my writings on several topics, ranging from books read, places travelled, technologies learnt and some notes on software. It has taken different shapes over the years. The content has been in blogger, wordpress, google sites, etc. So the site is also an avenue to try some new technology for me. The previous site (2015) was a static site built on Jekyll & bootstrap. It used “Clean blog” theme. The current website is a static site built on Hugo and it uses ‘Anatole’ theme. There was nothing wrong with Jekyll, but I wanted to try something different.
