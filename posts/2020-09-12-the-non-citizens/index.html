The non-citizens of the world
2020-09-09 00:00 folder General   1354 words schedule 6 min 9 s.

“Man is born free, yet everywhere he is in chains.” ~ Jean-Jacques Rousseau 1

The movie - C U Soon

Saw this amazing movie named C U Soon2 on Amazon Prime. The movie format was unique. Most of it was shown through video conferencing and chat. One gets to know the characters and their lives through chat messages and dialogues that happen through video apps. Chat and video have become the main medium to keep in touch with friends and family who are increasingly living in different parts of the world. That was portrayed beautifully in the movie. Clever movie making shows cinema as not merely imitating life but extending it.

As a viewer, you need to be more involved than a normal movie since you need to read the chat messages fast, look at the expressions of the faces of the characters as they talk to each other through Duo 3. Had to pause or rewind sometimes just to read the chat messages again, since some of them were malayalam sentences written in English. But that was not the mistake of the movie but that it just took me a bit to adjust to the fast pace. You also get to see the extreme multi-tasking that the young people of the current generation are used to (or subjected to). Jimmy, the main protagonist, is not only talking to his client at work but is also looking at the financial metrics and chatting with his girlfriend at the same time. It is like doing three things in parallel. Sometimes he even chats with his friend at the same time. Chat as a medium allows us to do multi-tasking, communicate with multiple people as well as do other things on the computer/phone.

Is multi-tasking good?

I’m not sure if it is a good thing or not. Not multi-tasking per se but extreme multi- tasking of doing so many things in tandem. Some work tasks do require more mental capacity and one needs to dedicate time for it. Are people forgoing complex tasks in order to do many simple tasks? Of course, there is a feel good factor when you check off things of your list. Doing many simple things and completing them would definitely give a major dopamine boost. But that also makes one less productive. If you’re continuously in ‘context-switching, split-second decision making’ situations, you tend to make only superficial judgements. Daniel Kahneman talks at length about this in Thinking, Fast and Slow. Not training System 2 (investing effort to check the intuition) and always relying on System 1 (intuition) can be disastrous. I think, in the movie, Jimmy makes a decision to marry a person that he sees online and interacts a few times, without doing much of due diligence and not paying attention to the red flags, because of impulsive decision making which could be a result of constant context switching.

Fahadh Faasil

I was super impressed by Fahadh. Every movie of his that I’ve seen in recent times, has been thought-provoking and I can’t but feel that he is a true genius. The last 3 movies of his that I saw - Trance, Natholi Oru Cheriya Meen Alla, and Kumbalangi Nights, were each unique and offered perspectives unlike anything else that I’d seen. Trance was probably one of his best movies, though I haven’t seen all of his movies yet. His movies cover so many aspects of human life that one can talk about each for ages. The movies cover wide ranging themes like love, addiction, religion, commercialization of spirituality, hypermasculinity, patriarchy, poverty, rural life, city life, day dreaming, communism, and so on. Yes, kudos to Fahadh for being the producer and playing the role of Kevin to perfection.

The key message

Though the movie depicted many facets of today’s life - online interactions, forming a relationship without meeting a person in person, living mostly indoors, ability to hack into someone’s digital profile to get information that they didn’t give, the shock of dealing with real life challenges, etc., the key take away, the moral of the story, so as to speak, was about human trafficking. People being duped with the promise of a job opportunity or better life prospects and taken to a different country to be made slaves, is the modern day abduction. Legally or illegally, if a person goes to another country and is trapped and abuse, and cannot talk about it, he/she becomes a non-citizen.

The unfortunates

Many people are in the ‘jumping out of the frying pan to the fire’ situation. They have come to that point in life probably because of the culmination of a series of misfortunes. It is like you’re at the edge of a cliff of despair. No money to live, poor parents, possibly a sick parent, mounting debts. You feel checkmated from all sides and the only option is to trust a stranger and go abroad for work. There are millions of people who are victims of trafficking world wide. My wife is in the board of a non-profit4 that deals with sexual trafficking and the stories that she’s told me always fills me with sadness about the human condition itself. But it is not that people are facing misfortunes, the biggest tragedy is that there are always people who take advantage of such people in dire situations. But the aggressors themselves might have been victims in the past.

Why does trafficking happen?

Though one could argue that there are always devilish people who want to harass others, I think the main reason is poverty and joblessness. The devil in the human comes out the worst during poverty or crushing sense of defeat. A lot of unrest in many parts of the world could be traced to poverty and the inability to break free of the vicious cycle.

There are so many philanthropists and organizations at work to help people out of poverty but the effort seems to be not moving the needle on a large scale.

What is the root cause of trafficking?

The root cause of trafficking itself is poverty, I think.

Virtue to vice or vice to Virtue?

In his essay “Discourse on the Origin and Foundations of Inequality among Men” Rousseau says that “people are endowed with innate virtue and, more importantly, the attributes of compassion and empathy”. His argument is that people move from virtue towards vice as they progress in life. it might be true for a single person and for humanity as a whole. I’m not sure if I totally agree with it or not. The Christian thought is that man is born in sin and can progress towards a life of virtue as he learns more and understands the nature of things better. I think the overarching theme of many Indian philosophies is also that through our life, we attain self-realization.

What is pushing people to poverty?

Due to environmental issues like floods, forest fires, and diseases, more people are pushed into poverty than ever before. Books like ‘The Better Angels of our Nature’ and ‘Factfulness’ do point out that the standard of living has become way better than a century ago. But I feel that it could have become even more better. The 70’s hoped for a utopia by the 2000s that never materialized.

Is there a solution?

It is possible to remove poverty with the advancement of technology. There has been a lot of advancement but not quite enough. covid-19 itself proved how far we are in terms of combating a potent virus. forest fires and floods are another problem too. People could get pushed to poverty through so many accidental things that might happen to them. If you look at places where there is lot of violence, they are also probably places that lack infrastructure or basic living conditions are not good enough. I hope for a day when world hunger could be solved by technological advances. Covid proved that govt can give money to each citizen if needed. Universal basic income is not such a remote concept after all.
